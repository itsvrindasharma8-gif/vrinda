The Problem

Stockouts were costing revenue invisibly

With 35,000+ SKUs across multiple categories, RedTape's inventory team was operating reactively replenishment decisions arrived after stockouts had already occurred. Fast-moving SKUs would fall below safe levels without warning, leading to lost sales, delayed fulfilment, and downstream logistics strain.

The ask was to build an automated monitoring dashboard that would surface at risk SKUs before stockout not after and eliminate the manual effort of checking hundreds of product lines daily.

Design Features

How the dashboard works

Dynamic Reorder Alerts

SKUs falling below calculated reorder points trigger colour-coded alerts amber for approaching, red for critical visible immediately on dashboard load.

Velocity-Based Thresholds

Reorder points are not static. They adapt to rolling 30-day sales velocity per SKU, ensuring seasonal fast-movers trigger alerts earlier than slow-moving lines.

Category Drill-Down

Users can filter by product category, warehouse location, or supplier enabling targeted replenishment decisions without scrolling through the full 35K+ SKU list.

Automated Refresh

Dashboard data refreshes on a scheduled basis, meaning the inventory team see accurate stock positions without any manual data exports or pivot table maintenance.

Technical Build

Under the hood

The core logic was built on SQL queries that joined inventory tables with sales velocity data, computing a rolling average daily sales rate per SKU and multiplying by a safety buffer (typically 7–14 days lead time) to derive the reorder point.

This was connected to a Power BI dataset with scheduled refresh, surfaced through a dashboard that non-technical stakeholders could read and act on without training. Alert logic was encoded directly in DAX measures, enabling colour rules and summary tables to update automatically.

Reactive restocking eliminated

After launch, the inventory team shifted from fire-fighting individual stockouts to proactive weekly planning driven by dashboard alerts. Fast-moving SKUs were replenished ahead of need, reducing fulfilment delays and enabling more confident promotional planning around peak periods.
