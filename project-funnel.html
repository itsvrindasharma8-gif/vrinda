Overview

What the project set out to solve

RedTape's e-commerce platform was generating significant traffic but converting a far smaller proportion than industry benchmarks suggested was possible. The task was to move beyond surface-level page metrics and conduct a granular, stage-by-stage funnel analysis to pinpoint exactly where users were abandoning their journeys and why.

Using 100,000+ monthly transaction records extracted via SQL, I built a behavioural segmentation model that mapped sessions from first touch to final purchase, flagging statistical anomalies at every step of the funnel.

The most significant finding was the 36% drop between cart and checkout nearly double the standard industry attrition at that stage. This was the critical intervention point.

Key Insights

What the data told us

Checkout Friction

Form abandonment was concentrated in the address and payment fields. Mobile users showed 2.3x higher exit rates at this stage than desktop users.

Payment Method Bias

COD selection caused a secondary abandonment loop users selecting COD converted 40% less than prepaid users, inflating RTO rates simultaneously.

Session Quality Signals

Users from organic search had 1.8x higher funnel completion vs paid traffic. SEO-driven sessions showed stronger purchase intent from first touch.

Category Patterns

Footwear had 12% better funnel completion than apparel. Price-point sensitivity analysis showed bundles outperformed individual SKUs by 18%.

Methodology

How the analysis was built

All data was extracted from the transactional database using advanced SQL queries CTEs for session sequencing, window functions for drop-off attribution, and aggregation joins across user_id, session_id, and event_type tables.

The output was visualised in Power BI with drill-through filters for device type, traffic source, product category, and geography enabling the product and UX teams to explore findings independently and act on granular segments.

Recommendations were prioritised using an effort-impact matrix, with the checkout UI redesign identified as the highest-leverage action.

+2.5% Conversion Rate After UI Changes

Post-implementation analysis confirmed that simplifying the checkout form (removing redundant fields, adding address autofill, and surfacing prepaid incentives earlier) lifted overall conversion by 2.5 percentage points translating to a measurable revenue impact across 2M+ monthly sessions.
